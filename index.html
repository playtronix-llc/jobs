Engineering Interview Handbook

At Playtronix, we evaluate candidates on broad distributed engineering skills and strong software engineering fundamentals; this guide applies to both frontend and backend interviews. Our interview framework focuses on problem-solving and learning agility, letting us see both depth and breadth. We want to understand not only how you code, but how you think. The process is structured in three stages, with interviews scheduled accordingly.

Stage-1 · Live coding round · 60 minutes · Online

Stage-1: Live coding round – 60 minutes | Online

In this interview, you’ll solve coding problems live using LeetCode’s online coding platform. You will share your screen with the interviewer throughout the session—no IDE setup is required.

This round will primarily focus on data structures and algorithms, so be prepared to solve problems involving arrays, strings, recursion, linked lists, trees, graphs, dynamic programming, and other core concepts.

Please ensure you have an active LeetCode account before the interview, as all coding will be done directly on LeetCode during the session.

Each interview is capped at 60 minutes and is designed to evaluate:

  • Problem-solving skills
  • Code quality and efficiency
  • Understanding of algorithms and data structures
  • Communication of thought process
Stage-2 · Tech round · 60 minutes · On-site

Stage-2: Tech round – 60 minutes | On-site interview

Note: This round is required to be conducted offline at our Belgrade office. Under exceptional circumstances, it may be run online via Google Meet and excalidraw.com for drawing system architecture.

This interview assesses your technical depth, breadth, and problem-solving through designing a solution to a defined problem. Your interviewer will evaluate your thought process, decision-making, and considerations such as performance and reliability. It varies between frontend and backend as follows:

Backend

It is a typical system design round. You’ll design a solution on a whiteboard or paper, starting with fundamentals and adding complexity. Asking clarifying questions early and clearly communicating your thinking is essential.

We’re interested in your approach, how you draw from past experience, and how you design with customers, scalability, and reliability in mind. You can also expect questions in the language of your speciality and some questions based on your previous experience.

Frontend

The session will include a live-coding segment. Tasks will be short and focused on practical skills— for example, code small utilities in JS/TS, solve a React component logic challenge, build a tiny state machine, or optimize a performance bottleneck.

We evaluate code clarity, ability to reason about edge cases, and how well you translate theoretical knowledge into practice. Please be prepared for in-depth theory questions across JS/TS, React and its ecosystem, System Design & Architecture, and Frontend Performance. We will focus on language fundamentals and React internals. Expect scenario-based reasoning rather than rote definitions.

Stage-3 · Behavioural round · 60 minutes · Online

Stage-3: Behavioural round – 60 minutes | Online

This interview includes a set of behavioural interview questions, aimed at assessing how your thought process and actions are aligned with our core set of company values.

In this round, we are keen to understand how you collaborate with your teams, cross-functional communication, previous projects, and to get a deeper sense of your ambitions to ensure the role is in fact the right one for you.

Our questions will be scenario-based, so please come prepared with examples that demonstrate your experience across one or more projects that you have been a part of or have led.
